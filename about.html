01.01 / Origin

Shipping apps before I had a degree.

I didn’t wait for graduation to start engineering products. By the time I received my Computer Science degree in 2024 (CGPA 8.72), I had already architected and shipped applications across five different companies. I started at Krishworks in April 2022, building Englishioh (an Arabic ↔ English fluency app for Saudi students) and contributing to Speaklifi. I mastered cross-language UX, RTL layouts, and TTS gamification before I even sat for my final exams.

01.02 / Rapid Scale

Five companies. Seven verticals. Millions of users.

My early career was a crucible of diverse verticals and strict deadlines. I built a veterinary adoption marketplace at Lottle before the startup wound down. At Dropdown, I shipped Vibey's Flutter Web platform across four cities. Then, during a grueling ten-month stint at Indomitech, I shipped six live applications: Ayla Voice Chat (Agora-backed live rooms), Olio (a hyperlocal marketplace scaling to 5M+ downloads), JagoBd (an Android TV OTT app with a 10M+ install base), QuickDock (telemedicine), FuturePeak (EdTech), and an alumni network of 5,000+ users. The scale of the userbase became my ultimate testing ground.

01.03 / Medjeex

Building the entire mobile stack from zero.

In October 2024, I joined Medjeex as SDE-1. There was effectively no mobile codebase. Over the next ~1.5 years, I architected and shipped four distinct applications end-to-end: the core NEET/JEE student app, a parent companion app, an internal QR attendance system, and a teacher console. The student app spans 22 feature modules, integrating a Mediasoup SFU live-class stack, an offline-first self-test engine on Isar, and an AI mentor pipeline processing over 2,000+ lecture recordings. This isn't just UI work—it's heavy systems engineering.

01.04 / Philosophy

Receipts over rhetoric.

I refuse to pad my resume with vague claims like "improved engagement by 30%." If I can't link to the live app or the real diff, I don't claim the metric. Every application listed here is either live on the Play Store/App Store, or I explicitly state the company wound down. I value raw, verifiable execution over polished rhetoric. The code either ships to real users, or it doesn't. That is the only standard that matters.

01.05 / Next

Seeking hard problems at product companies.

I am actively mastering Low-Level Design (LLD) and advanced DSA (250+ on TakeUForward) to tackle increasingly complex architectures. I am targeting SDE roles at product companies and GCCs where performance, offline capabilities, and real-time data sync actually matter. I am not interested in building simple chat wrappers or animation demos. If you are hiring for deep systems engineering, we should talk.

01.06 / How I work 03 principles

Three operating rules / I won't break.

01

Receipts Over Rhetoric

If I can't link to a live app or a verifiable metric, I don't claim it. No padded stats. No vanity metrics. The work either ships to production or it doesn't.

02

From initial auth flows to App Store release. I own the architecture, API contracts, local persistence, payments, and OTA updates. I treat the entire surface area as my responsibility.
